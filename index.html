Seniority Total Loss Rates ⓘ

Two independent risk dimensions. Seniority rates reflect capital stack position (structural protection). Sector rates reflect cyclical/macro exposure. They combine multiplicatively:

Combined = 1 − (1 − seniority) × (1 − sector)

Example: 5% seniority + 10% sector = 14.5% combined loss — not 15%. This avoids double-counting since the two dimensions are independent.

Capital Structure Waterfall ⓘ

How losses flow through the capital stack. Investments are split into encumbered (pledged to secured credit facilities) and unencumbered (free) pools.

Secured lenders claim encumbered assets first. Excess encumbered assets flow to the general pool. If encumbered assets fall short, the secured shortfall becomes pari passu with unsecured noteholders against unencumbered assets.

Unsecured recovery shows cents on the dollar available to unsecured bondholders after all senior claims are satisfied.

Encumbered/unencumbered splits from iXBRL footnote data. Losses applied proportionally across both pools.

Encumbrance assumption. We conservatively estimate that all assets marked as “encumbered” or “pledged” in SEC filings are fully encumbered—i.e., 100% of their fair value is pledged to secured credit facilities.

This is likely an overstatement. In practice, credit facility borrowing bases typically apply advance rates (e.g., 50–70% of fair value), meaning a significant portion of “encumbered” assets may have residual unencumbered value available to unsecured creditors. The true encumbered fraction is almost certainly lower than shown.

Implied NAV/Share ⓘ
--

Post-stress NAV per share. Portfolio losses flow through leverage directly to equity. Equity residual = total assets − portfolio loss − all liabilities. Implied NAV/share = equity residual / shares outstanding.

The leverage multiplier means a 10% portfolio loss can translate to a 20-30% equity loss depending on the debt/equity ratio.

Current PIK Exposure ⓘ

Payment-in-Kind (PIK). Instead of paying cash interest, the borrower adds accrued interest to the loan principal. PIK increases exposure over time without generating cash flow.

High PIK concentration signals credit stress — borrowers that can't service cash interest. PIK positions also grow the portfolio's funded exposure, increasing loss severity in a downturn.

Current Non-Accrual Exposure ⓘ

Non-accrual means the BDC has stopped recognizing interest income on the loan, typically because the borrower has missed payments or is expected to default.

Non-accrual status is disclosed via footnotes in the Schedule of Investments. These positions are already marked down (fair value < cost) and represent the portfolio's most distressed credits.

Liquidity Assumptions

Unfunded Draw Rate ⓘ%

Unfunded Draw Rate — % of total unfunded commitments (revolvers, delayed draw term loans, equity commitments) assumed to be drawn by borrowers during stress.

In a downturn, distressed borrowers draw down credit lines, forcing the BDC to fund new loans at par into a declining portfolio. Higher draw rates mean more cash outflow and larger balance sheet expansion at the worst time.

Forced Sale Disc. ⓘ%

Forced Sale Discount — haircut applied if assets must be liquidated quickly to raise cash.

Middle-market loans are illiquid. In stress, forced sellers receive well below fair value. A 15% discount means $1B of assets yields only $850M in cash — the liquidity premium for fire-sale conditions.

Dividend Rate ⓘ%

Dividend Rate — annual dividend as % of post-stress equity.

BDCs must distribute 90%+ of taxable income to maintain RIC tax status. This is a mandatory cash drain that can't easily be stopped even in stress. The rate is applied to post-stress equity, so larger portfolio losses automatically reduce the dividend burden.

Asset Coverage Ratio (Post-Stress) ⓘ

Asset coverage ratio = total assets / total borrowings. BDCs must maintain at least 150% by regulation (200% prior to 2019). Most credit facilities impose 200% covenants.

Falling below 150% triggers regulatory action. Below 200% may trigger covenant defaults, accelerating debt repayment and potentially forcing asset sales at distressed prices.

Red line = 150% regulatory minimum. Amber = 200% typical covenant.

Net Liquidity Position

Cash + undrawn revolver capacity (adjusted for renewal risk and borrowing base shrinkage) − stressed unfunded draws − near-term debt maturities (2yr).

Unfunded Commitment Stress ⓘ

Unfunded commitments are contractual obligations to lend additional capital (revolvers, delayed draw term loans, equity commitments) that borrowers haven't drawn yet.

In a downturn, distressed borrowers draw down revolvers and delayed draws, forcing the BDC to fund at par into a declining portfolio. The "draw rate" models what percentage of unfunded commitments get called.
