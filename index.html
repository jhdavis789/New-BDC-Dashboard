Loans maturing within the repricing window are excluded from stress losses and treated as cash recovered at par.

The assumption: maturing loans get repaid and redeployed at efficiently priced terms given current market awareness. Only loans maturing after the window are subject to the modeled loss rates.

Positions without a maturity date (equity, preferred, JV) are always fully exposed regardless of this setting.

Capital Structure Waterfall

Assumptions: Secured lenders have first claim on encumbered (ringfenced) assets only. If encumbered assets exceed secured debt, the excess flows to the general pool. If encumbered assets are insufficient, the secured shortfall becomes a pari passu claim alongside unsecured noteholders against the general (unencumbered) pool. Losses applied proportionally across both pools. Encumbered/unencumbered splits from iXBRL footnote data.
